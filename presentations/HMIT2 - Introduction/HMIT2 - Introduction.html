MVP Thinking

Amazon

Amazon MVP

  • Problem to solve: How can people find and buy the exact book they want, even if a local bookstore does not have it?
  • Why books?
  • Huge catalog → huge potential customer base, predictable demand
  • Simple logistics → simple to store, pack and ship
  • Standardized, predictable product → felt less risky to buy, low return rates

Today Amazon solves a much bigger problem: Buy everything online.

Problem Harvest

List 20 small frustrations people experience in everyday life

  • no solutions
  • no app ideas
  • only annoying, repeated, real situations

Questions you might ask

  • “What wastes time?”
  • “What is confusing?”
  • “What do people forget?”
  • “What causes coordination problems?”
  • “What feels unnecessarily hard?”
  • “What do people do with screenshots, notes, or WhatsApp because no better tool exists?”

Pick your top candidates and write a problem statement for each of them. A clear problem statement follows the pattern:

“\[Who\] struggles to \[job\] because of \[obstacle\], leading to \[undesired outcome\]”

Example: “Remote teams struggle to stay aligned and communicate efficiently because of fragmented tools and endless email threads, leading to misunderstandings, slower decisions, and reduced team productivity” (Slack)
